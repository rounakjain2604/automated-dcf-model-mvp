DCF Guide for Non-Finance Users

This explains what the model is doing, why it matters, and how to interpret the outputs.

1) What this model answers

  • Goal: estimate what a business is worth today using expected future cash flows.
  • Core idea: future cash is worth less than cash today, so we discount it back.
  • Main output: enterprise value, equity value, and implied price per share.

2) Inputs you provide

  • Revenue and EBITDA (or upload historical financials).
  • Growth rate assumptions (how fast business grows).
  • WACC / discount rate (required return).
  • Terminal growth and capital structure (cash, debt).

3) What the engine calculates

  • Builds 5-year forecast for revenue, margins, reinvestment, and working capital.
  • Computes unlevered free cash flow (cash available to all investors).
  • Discounts each year using WACC (with mid-year convention).
  • Computes terminal value via Gordon and Exit Multiple methods.
  • Uses a blended terminal value for a more balanced result.

4) Quality controls built in

  • Terminal growth is capped by GDP-style long-run guardrails.
  • Minimum spread between WACC and terminal growth prevents unstable perpetuity math.
  • Check tab flags balance/sanity issues and terminal FCF concerns.
  • Sensitivity table shows valuation changes under different WACC and growth assumptions.

5) How to read outputs

  • Valuation sheet: detailed DCF math and implied prices.
  • Dashboard sheet: quick summary for decision makers.
  • Sensitivity sheet: best/worst case valuation ranges.
  • Word report: narrative summary and charts for sharing.

6) Practical workflow

  • Open portal at http://127.0.0.1:5000.
  • Select scenario (Base/Bull/Bear), fill assumptions or upload file.
  • Generate pack and review Dashboard + Checks first.
  • Adjust assumptions, rerun, and compare with ask/current price.
